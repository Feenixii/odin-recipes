Mexican Chile Rellenos

What is it..?

Latin America's take on stuffed peppers, chile rellenos is typically made using poblano peppers, but other peppers can be used instead if you can't find them. The poblano is a really nice pepper that's not particularly spicy, but packs a powerful flavor punch. Traditionally, they are first roasted, then stuffed with cheese and coated in a fluffy egg batter; fried until golden brown and served over salsa. You can add additional stuffings like meat, rice, or beans to spice them up!

Ingredients

  • 6 large poblano peppers
  • 8 ounces Monterey Jack cheese, cut into long strips or shredded
  • 4 cups oil, for frying
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup all-pourpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp salt

Directions

  1. Roast the peppers on a baking sheet by placing directly on the top rack under the broiler for about 5 minutes or until the skin is blackened and blistered. Flip them over and broil another 5 minutes.
  2. Remove from the oven and cover with foil to let them steam for 5 minutes, then peel the skin from the peppers.
  3. Cut a small slit down the middle of the pepper to allow them to be stuffed. If you don't want the peppers too spicy, remove the seeds.
  4. Stuff the peppers with cheese carefully. They will be delicate from the roasting process.
  5. Gently close the openings of the peppers and secure it shut with a few toothpicks. Use as many toothpicks as is necessary to ensure the cheese won't ooze out during the frying process.
  6. Heat the oil in a large sauce pan or deep skillet over medium-high heat. While the oil is heating, prepare the batter.
  7. Separate the egg whites from the yolks in 2 bowls. Beat the egg whites until stiff peaks form. While continuing to beat the whites, add in the yolks one at a time until they're fully incorporated. The batter should be light, fluffy, and smooth.
  8. Mix the flour and salt together in a shallow bowl and coat the peppers. Then dip them in the egg batter and place gently in the hot frying oil.
  9. Fry the peppers for 3-5 minutes per side or until the batter is golden brown and crispy. Transfer to a paper towel lined baking sheet to remove excess oil.
  10. Serve on a plate over salsa and enjoy!
